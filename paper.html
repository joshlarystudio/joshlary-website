In my early 20s, I was obsessed with creating geometric patterns. I drew thousands of them on bar napkins, sketchbooks, and really any piece of paper I could find.

About a year ago, I discovered these drawings while cleaning out my childhood room. Eight months ago, I decided to recreate them using construction paper I’d accumulated over the years.

These patterns mark a time in my life where I was incapable of changing my behavior and life became a pattern of bad decisions followed by even worse consequences.

This series highlights my behavioral patterns (old and new), showing which have begun to change and which I’ve continued to struggle with since getting sober.
